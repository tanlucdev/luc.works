## Overview

This portfolio presents Tan Luc as a frontend engineer, product builder, and AI workflow developer focused on AI-powered products, workflow systems, and thoughtful digital experiences.

The repository consistently frames the work around a design-first mindset, clean architecture, modern web technologies, and products that help people focus, create, and work better.

## Product Belief

The clearest philosophy statement in the repository is: great software should feel invisible. The supporting copy explains that good products remove friction rather than add features, helping people think clearly, move faster, and focus on meaningful work.

This philosophy appears in the homepage `PhilosophyMotion` component and is reinforced by the About and Hero sections.

## Engineering Belief

The resume page and project metadata emphasize frontend architecture, TypeScript, design systems, AI workflow systems, and product development.

The repository implementation supports this direction through Astro pages, React islands for motion, centralized TypeScript project data, Tailwind styling, and a documented visual design system.

## Design Belief

The design system favors a black canvas, warm ivory typography, sparse panels, restrained motion, large display type, and compact body copy. The design documentation describes the interface as quiet, cinematic, product-focused, content-first, and premium minimal.

The visual style supports the written philosophy by reducing decoration and giving content, pacing, and hierarchy most of the attention.

## AI Workflow Belief

The project content repeatedly points toward AI workflow systems rather than isolated AI features. CinematicAI is framed around node-based workflows, AI orchestration, repeatable creative pipelines, and visible execution states.

The repository evidence suggests a preference for AI tools that are inspectable, reusable, and understandable to users, especially when workflows become complex.

## UX Belief

The featured projects show a consistent interest in reducing friction:

- CinematicAI focuses on making creative AI pipelines repeatable and understandable.
- CafeMaps focuses on quicker, more trustworthy cafe discovery.
- Study Stream focuses on shared accountability without distracting users from focus.
- Engineering Focus describes component boundaries, maintainable styling, accessible interfaces, and pragmatic AI-assisted workflows.

## Content Belief

The audit documents recommend deeper, evidence-backed content. They specifically warn against shallow summaries, unsupported outcomes, and project pages that do not explain problem, users, architecture, decisions, trade-offs, results, and lessons.

The portfolio philosophy should therefore stay factual: explain the work clearly, use repository evidence where possible, and avoid invented achievements or metrics.

## Working Principles

- Build interfaces that help people stay focused on their goal.
- Use architecture to make complex products easier to understand and change.
- Treat design systems as practical constraints, not decoration.
- Make AI workflows inspectable instead of hiding complexity behind a prompt box.
- Prefer clear product decisions over broad claims.
- Keep portfolio content honest when evidence is limited.

## Future Direction

The repository is ready for deeper content before UI expansion. The next strong step is to connect each featured project to factual problem statements, system models, technical decisions, product decisions, trade-offs, and lessons learned.

Writing should support that direction by explaining judgment behind the projects, especially around AI workflows, frontend architecture, product UX, design systems, and pragmatic AI tooling.
